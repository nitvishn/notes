Dynamics

Definitions

  • Inertia is resistance an object has to a change of velocity.
  • Mass is a numerical measure of:
    1. the inertia of a body
    2. the amount of matter in an object.
  • Weight is the gravitational force acting on an object.

Formulae

F=ma  ⟹  W=mg \begin{aligned} F&=ma\\ \implies W &= mg\\[1em] \end{aligned} F⟹W​=ma=mg​

Concepts

  • Forces have two types:
    • Contact: Forces exerted by objects actually touching each other
    • Non-contact (long range): Forces exerted without objects touching (gravity, electromagnetic)
  • Equilibrium:
    • Conditions for equilibrium:
      • The net force acting on it is 000.
      • The net moment it experiences is 000.
    • An object in equilibrum might either be stationary or moving at constant velocity.

Different forces

  • Tension is the force that arises when a body is streched.

  • Reaction/normal/perpendicular force
  • Drag force:
    • Forces that oppose the motion of a body through a fluid
    • Directed opposite to the velocity of a body
