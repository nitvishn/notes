Definitions

  1. An oscillation is a cyclical (repeating) motion of an object. If the motion repeats in a fixed time interval it is called isochronous, and the time interval of repetition is called the period. An oscillation will have limits on its displacement from a central point.
  2. Simple harmonic motion is defined as motion arising from acceleration of an object that is proportional to its displacement from a fixed equilibrium point (rest position) and directed toward that point.F,a∝−xF,a \propto -xF,a∝−x
  3. A restoring force (F)(F)(F) is any force that is proportional to the opposite of a displacement, which is responsible for bringing an object in SHM back to its equilibrium.
  4. The amplitude of SHM is the maximum displacement from equilibrium.
  5. Two sinusoidal curves of equal period have a phase shift that translates one curve relative to the other in time, expressed as a fraction of a cycle, or as an angle in radians or degrees.
