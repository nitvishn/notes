Demand & Supply

Definitions

  • Demand: the ability and willingness of an individual to buy a product at a certain price in a specific time period.
  • Supply: the ability and willingness of a firm to provide/sell a product at a certain price during a specific time period.
  • Law of demand:
    • there is a negative causal relationship between demand and price.
    • when the price of a product increases, quantity demanded decreases.
    • when the price of a product decreases, the quantity demanded increases.
    • ceteris paribus.

Demand

  • Factors affecting demand:
    • Price.
    • Advertising.
    • Price of complements.
    • Price of substitutes.
    • Interest rates.
    • Taxes[Direct]

  • The general equation for a demand curve is:

      • −1b-\frac{1}{b}−b1​ is the gradient.
    • aaa determines the xxx and yyy intercept of the curve, and effectively, the position of the curve. Increasing the value of aaa by uuu units causes a rightward shift of the curve by uuu units, whereas decreasing the value of aaa by uuu units causes a leftward shift of the demand curve by uuu units.
    • bbb determines the gradient of the demand curve and dictates whether the product is relatively price elastic or inelastic in demand. Decreasing the value of bbb increases the gradient of the curve, making it more steep, whereas increasing the value of bbb decreases the gradient of the curve, making it less steep.
