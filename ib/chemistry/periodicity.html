Effective nuclear charge and the shielding effect

  • The reduction of effective nuclear charge due to the repulsion of inner electrons is called shielding.
  • The nuclear charge is given by the number of protons and it increases by one between successive elements as a proton is added to the nucleus.´

Periodicity

  • ‘Periodicity’ refers to the specific trend/patterns that emerge down a group and across a period.
  • Electrons play the biggest part in determining the physical and chemical properties of an element.

Metals

  • Reactivity refers to how easily metals donate electrons.
  • For metals, reactivity increases down a group.
  • Metallic character decreases while moving across a period.
  • The first group elements donate 1 electron from the S orbital, consequently, less energy is required.

Ionisation Energy

  • We study ionisation energy to understand how easily metals lose electrons.
  • Ionisation energy decreases down the group.

Oxides

  • Change from basic through amphoteric to acidic across a period.
  • When elements bond with oxygen, oxides are formed.
  • Ionic compounds are formed between a metal and a nonmetal so the oxides of Na, Mg, and Al have giant ionic structures. (High M.P)
  • Covalent compounds are formed between non-metals so the oxides of P, S, and Cl are molecular covalent. (Low M.P)
  • The oxide of the metalloid silicon has a giant covalent structure. (High M.P)

Electronegativity and Ionic Character

  • The ionic character of a compound depends upon the difference in electronegativity between its elements.
  • Oxygen has an electronegativity of 3.5 so the ionic character of the oxides decreases from left to right as the electronegativity values of the Period 3 elements approach this value.
  • The oxides become more ionic down a group as electronegativity decreases.
  • Electrical conductivity is a measure of ionic character and can only be measured in the molten state when electrons are free to move.

Acid-Base Character

  • Metallic elements form basic oxides.
  • Non-metallic elements form covalent or non-metallic oxides which are acidic.
  • Na and Mg oxides are basic.
  • Al and Si oxides are amphoteric – they show both acidic and basic properties.
  • P, S, and Cl oxides are acidic
