# Circular Functions ## Fundamental identities $$ \begin{aligned} \sin^2{\theta} + \cos^2{\theta} &= 1\\[1em] 1 + \cot^2{\theta} &= \cosec^2{\theta}\\[1em] 1 + \tan^2{\theta} &= \sec^2{\theta} \end{aligned} $$ ## Compound angle formulae $$ \begin{aligned} \cos{(A+B)} &= \cos{A}\cos{B} - \sin{A}\sin{B} \\[1em] \cos{(A-B)} &= \cos{A}\cos{B} + \sin{A}\sin{B} \\[2.5em] \sin{(A + B)} &= \sin{A}\cos{B} + \sin{B}\cos{A} \\[1em] \sin{(A - B)} &= \sin{A}\cos{B} - \sin{B}\cos{A} \\[2.5em] \tan{(A+B)} &=\frac{\tan{A} + \tan{B}}{1- \tan{A}\tan{B}} \\[1.5em] \tan{(A-B)} &= \frac{\tan{A} - \tan{B}}{1 + \tan{A}\tan{B}} \\[1em] \end{aligned} $$ ## Double angle formulae $$ \begin{aligned} \cos{2\theta} &= \cos^2{\theta} - \sin^2{\theta} \\[1em] &= 1 - 2\sin^2{\theta} \\[1em] &= 2\cos^2{\theta} - 1 \\[2em] \sin{2\theta} &= 2\sin{\theta}\cos{\theta} \\[1em] \tan{2\theta} &= \frac{2\tan{\theta}}{1-\tan^2{\theta}} \\[1em] \end{aligned} $$ ## Triple angle formulae $$ \begin{aligned} \cos{3\theta} = 4\cos^3{\theta} - 3\cos{\theta}\\[1em] \sin{3\theta} = 3\sin{\theta} - 4\sin^3{\theta}\\[1em] \tan{3\theta} = \frac{3\tan{\theta} - \tan^3{\theta}}{1-3\tan^2{\theta}}\\[1em] \end{aligned} $$ ## Half angle formulae $$ \begin{aligned} \cos{\frac{\theta}{2}} = \pm\sqrt{\frac{1+\cos{\theta}}{2}}\\[1em] \sin{\frac{\theta}{2}} = \pm\sqrt{\frac{1-\cos{\theta}}{2}}\\[1em] \tan{\frac{\theta}{2}} = \pm\sqrt{\frac{1-\cos{\theta}}{1+\cos{\theta}}} \\[1em] \end{aligned} $$ ## Standard angles | | $0$ | $30$ | $45$ | $60$ | $90$ | |--|--|--|--|--|--| | $\sin$ | $0$ | $\frac{1}{2}$ | $\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$ |$\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ | $1$ | | $\cos$ | $1$ | $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ | $\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$ | $\frac{1}{2}$ | $0$ | | $\tan$ | $0$ | $\frac{1}{\sqrt3}$ | 1 | $\sqrt3$ | $-$ | ## R-formulae - If an equation is given in the form: $$a\cos{\theta} + b\sin{\theta} = c$$ - We can use the R-formulae to solve it. $$ \begin{aligned} a\cos{\theta} + b\sin{\theta} &= R\sin{(\theta + \alpha)}\\[1em] a\sin{\theta}+b\cos{\theta} &= R\sin{\theta}\cos{\alpha} + R\sin{\alpha}\cos{\theta}\\[1em] a &= R\cos{\alpha}\\[1em] b &= R\sin{\alpha}\\[1em] \frac{R\sin{\alpha}}{R\cos{\alpha}} &= \frac{b}{a}\\[1em] \tan{\alpha} &= \frac{b}{a}\\[1em] \alpha &= \tan^{-1}{(\frac{b}{a})}\\[1em] R\cos{\alpha} &= a\\[1em] R^2\cos^2{\alpha} &= a^2\\[1em] R\sin{\alpha} &= b\\[1em] R^2\sin^2{\alpha} &= b^2\\[1em] R^2\sin{\alpha} + R^2\cos^2{\alpha} &= a^2 + b^2\\[1em] R^2(\sin^2{\alpha} + \cos^2{\alpha}) &= a^2 + b^2\\[1em] R^2 &= a^2 + b^2\\[1em] R &= \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}\\[1em] \end{aligned} $$ **Note**: $R > 0$. - In general, the R-formulae are: $$ \begin{aligned} a\cos{\theta} + b\sin{\theta} &= R\sin{(\theta + \alpha)}\\[1em] a\cos{\theta} - b\sin{\theta} &= R\sin{(\theta - \alpha)}\\[1em] a\sin{\theta} + b\cos{\theta} &= R\cos{(\theta - \alpha)}\\[1em] a\sin{\theta} - b\cos{\theta} &= R\cos{(\theta + \alpha)}\\[1em] R &= \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}\\[1em] \alpha &= \tan^{-1}{(\frac{b}{a})}\\[1em] \end{aligned} $$ ## T-substitution method $$ \begin{aligned} \tan{2A} &= \frac{2\tan{A}}{1-\tan^2{A}} \\[1em] \text{let } A &= \frac{\theta}{2} \\[1em] \tan{2(\frac{\theta}{2})} &= \frac{2\tan{(\frac{\theta}{2}})}{1-\tan^2{(\frac{\theta}{2}})} \\[1em] \tan{\theta} &= \frac{2\tan{(\frac{\theta}{2}})}{1-\tan^2{(\frac{\theta}{2}})} \\[1em] \text{let } \tan{\frac{\theta}{2}} &= t\\[1em] \tan{\theta} &= \frac{2t}{1-t^2}\\[1em] \sin{\theta} &= \frac{2t}{1+ t^2}\\[1em] \cos{\theta} &= \frac{1-t^2}{1+ t^2}\\[1em] \end{aligned} $$ ## Sum to product formulae $$ \begin{aligned} \sin{(A+B)} + \sin{(A-B)} &= 2\sin{A}\cos{B}\\ \sin{(A+B)} - \sin{(A-B)} &= 2\cos{A}\sin{B}\\ \cos{(A+B)} + \cos{(A-B)} &= -2\sin{A}\sin{B}\\ \cos{(A+B)} - \cos{(A-B)} &= 2\cos{A}\cos{B}\\ \end{aligned} $$ ## Factor formulae $$ \begin{aligned} \sin{S} + \sin{D} &= 2\sin{(\frac{S +D}{2})}\cos{(\frac{S-D}{2})}\\[1em] \sin{S} - \sin{D} &= 2\cos{(\frac{S +D}{2})}\sin{(\frac{S-D}{2})}\\[1em] \cos{S} + \cos{D} &= 2\cos{(\frac{S +D}{2})}\cos{(\frac{S-D}{2})}\\[1em] \cos{S} - \cos{D} &= -2\sin{(\frac{S +D}{2})}\sin{(\frac{S-D}{2})}\\[1em] \end{aligned} $$ ## Restricted Trignometric Functions |Function| Restricted Domain | Restricted Range| Inverse Function| Domain|Range| |--|--|--|--|--|--| | $y = Sin(x)$ | $-\frac{\pi}{2}\leq x \leq \frac{\pi}{2}$ | $1 \leq y \leq 1$ | $y = \arcsin(x)$ | $1 \leq x \leq 1$ | $-\frac{\pi}{2}\leq y \leq \frac{\pi}{2}$ | | $y = Cos(x)$ | $0 \leq x \leq \pi$ | $1 \leq y \leq 1$ | $y = \arccos(x)$ | $1 \leq x \leq 1$ | $0 \leq y \leq \pi$ | | $y = Tan(x)$ | $-\frac{\pi}{2} < x < \frac{\pi}{2}$ | $y \in \R$ | $y = \arctan(x)$| $x \in \R$ | $-\frac{\pi}{2} < y < \frac{\pi}{2}$ |
