R-formulae

  • If an equation is given in the form:
    acos⁡θ+bsin⁡θ=ca\cos{\theta} + b\sin{\theta} = cacosθ+bsinθ=c

  • We can use the R-formulae to solve it.
    acos⁡θ+bsin⁡θ=Rsin⁡(θ+α)asin⁡θ+bcos⁡θ=Rsin⁡θcos⁡α+Rsin⁡αcos⁡θa=Rcos⁡αb=Rsin⁡αRsin⁡αRcos⁡α=batan⁡α=baα=tan⁡−1(ba)Rcos⁡α=aR2cos⁡2α=a2Rsin⁡α=bR2sin⁡2α=b2R2sin⁡α+R2cos⁡2α=a2+b2R2(sin⁡2α+cos⁡2α)=a2+b2R2=a2+b2R=a2+b2 \begin{aligned} a\cos{\theta} + b\sin{\theta} &= R\sin{(\theta + \alpha)}\\[1em] a\sin{\theta}+b\cos{\theta} &= R\sin{\theta}\cos{\alpha} + R\sin{\alpha}\cos{\theta}\\[1em] a &= R\cos{\alpha}\\[1em] b &= R\sin{\alpha}\\[1em] \frac{R\sin{\alpha}}{R\cos{\alpha}} &= \frac{b}{a}\\[1em] \tan{\alpha} &= \frac{b}{a}\\[1em] \alpha &= \tan^{-1}{(\frac{b}{a})}\\[1em] R\cos{\alpha} &= a\\[1em] R^2\cos^2{\alpha} &= a^2\\[1em] R\sin{\alpha} &= b\\[1em] R^2\sin^2{\alpha} &= b^2\\[1em] R^2\sin{\alpha} + R^2\cos^2{\alpha} &= a^2 + b^2\\[1em] R^2(\sin^2{\alpha} + \cos^2{\alpha}) &= a^2 + b^2\\[1em] R^2 &= a^2 + b^2\\[1em] R &= \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}\\[1em] \end{aligned} acosθ+bsinθasinθ+bcosθabRcosαRsinα​tanααRcosαR2cos2αRsinαR2sin2αR2sinα+R2cos2αR2(sin2α+cos2α)R2R​=Rsin(θ+α)=Rsinθcosα+Rsinαcosθ=Rcosα=Rsinα=ab​=ab​=tan−1(ab​)=a=a2=b=b2=a2+b2=a2+b2=a2+b2=a2+b2​​
    Note: R>0R > 0R>0.

  • In general, the R-formulae are:
    acos⁡θ+bsin⁡θ=Rsin⁡(θ+α)acos⁡θ−bsin⁡θ=Rsin⁡(θ−α)asin⁡θ+bcos⁡θ=Rcos⁡(θ−α)asin⁡θ−bcos⁡θ=Rcos⁡(θ+α)R=a2+b2α=tan⁡−1(ba) \begin{aligned} a\cos{\theta} + b\sin{\theta} &= R\sin{(\theta + \alpha)}\\[1em] a\cos{\theta} - b\sin{\theta} &= R\sin{(\theta - \alpha)}\\[1em] a\sin{\theta} + b\cos{\theta} &= R\cos{(\theta - \alpha)}\\[1em] a\sin{\theta} - b\cos{\theta} &= R\cos{(\theta + \alpha)}\\[1em] R &= \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}\\[1em] \alpha &= \tan^{-1}{(\frac{b}{a})}\\[1em] \end{aligned} acosθ+bsinθacosθ−bsinθasinθ+bcosθasinθ−bcosθRα​=Rsin(θ+α)=Rsin(θ−α)=Rcos(θ−α)=Rcos(θ+α)=a2+b2​=tan−1(ab​)​
