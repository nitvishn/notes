Thinking and Decision-making

  • Thinking is the cognitive process of using knowledge and information to make plans, interpret and model the word, and constructively interact with and make predictions about the world in general.
  • Decision making is a cognitive process that involves selecting one of the possible beliefs or actions, i.e. making a choice between alternatives.

Models of thinking and decision-making:

Normative models

  • Formal logic
    • a system of logic (as Aristotelian logic or symbolic logic) that abstracts the forms of thought from its content to establish abstract criteria of consistency
  • Utility theory
    • Rational decision-maker should calculate the expected utility for each option and then choose the option that maximizes this utility
      • Utility is the degree to which something helps us to achieve our goals

Descriptive models

Theory of reasoned action

  • Aim to explain the relationship between attitudes and behaviors when making choices.
  • Proposed by Martin Fishbein in 1967
  • Individual’s choice of a particular behavior is based on the expected outcome of that behavior.
    • Believing a certain behavior will lead to a certain outcome is a predisposition known as behavioral intention.
      • The stronger the behavioral intention, the stronger the effort we put into implementing the plan
  • 2 factors that determine behavioral intention
    • Attitudes
      • Describes your individual perception of the behavior.
    • Subjective norms
      • Describes the perceived social pressure regarding this behavior.
  • Depending on the situation, attitudes and subjective norms might have varying degrees of importance in determining the intention

Theory of planned behavior

  • Is an extension of the TRA
  • Proposed in 1985
  • Introduces third factor that influences behavioral intention; Perceived behavioral control
    • Added to account for situations in which the attitude is positive, and the subjective norms do not prevent from performing the behavior; however, you do not think you are able to carry out the action.

Studies that can be used to explain TRA/TPB:
  • Ajzen and Fishbein (1973)
    • Meta-analysis of published research
    • Reports a 0.63 correlation between intentions and behavior
  • Albarracin et al (2001)
    • Meta-analysis of TRA and TPB as models of condom use
      • TRA and TPB are successful predictors of condom use
      • The average correlation between intention and behavior is these models in 0.51
      • “Thus, people are more likely to use condoms if they have previously formed the corresponding intentions. These intentions to use condoms appear to derive from attitudes, subjective norms and perceived behavioral control.”
